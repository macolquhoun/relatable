How are things calculated?

Cousins

Your cousins are calculated based on the distance from your grandparents. For example, if you both share a grandparent you are first cousins. If you both share a great grandparent you are second cousins, great great grandparents are you're third cousins etc.

Removed

The word removed is used to describe the distance between a relationship. For example, you and your cousin are both two generations away from your common ancestor ( your grandparent ), which means that "removed" is not needed here. However, your cousin's son is three generations from your common ancestor ( your grandparent ) so they become your first cousin once removed. Their son would be twice removed, and the list goes on.

Figuring out the removed relations can get tricky and that's where the calculator comes in handy. Just tell us your common relatives and we'll do all the hard work for you.
